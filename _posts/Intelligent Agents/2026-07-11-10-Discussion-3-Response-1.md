Bias leading to inaccurate predictions is a great point. This could affect content recommendation systems in particular, potentially promoting content that reinforces biases learned in training, leading to social division (Sonni et al., 2024).

The current uncertainty of copyright of AI generated work is a real barrier to adoption in some industries. Large media outlets for example are concerned about lawsuits stemming from the unknown copyright status of the training material used for image generation services. This can be mitigated by services like Adobe Firefly and Getty Generative AI which use proprietary training material (Thomson, Thomas and Matich, 2025).

Combatting AI generated misinformation remains an unsolved problem, deep learning may be able to help (Thomson, Thomas and Matich, 2025), but others in the media space see fact checking as an inherently human activity (Cools and Diakopoulos, 2026). The last point bothers me greatly, if fact checking cannot be meaningfully automated, then it may become increasingly expensive to sort fact from fiction (Wellbrock, 2024).
