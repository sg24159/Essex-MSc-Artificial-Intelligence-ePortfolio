The ethical issues of generative deep learning touch many fields. The field where I have the greatest concerns about generated content is journalism.

First, it’s worth pointing out that automated news writing is not new, the Associated Press was machine generating financial reports in 2014 (Sonni et al., 2024). Modern editorial guidelines emphasize transparency, accountability, and responsibility in their AI policies, but guidelines are lacking around legal compliance and algorithmic bias (Thomson, Thomas and Matich, 2025). However, smaller outlets do not have the same guidelines, and may be more likely to use generative AI (Thomson, Thomas and Matich, 2025).

The use of AI in writing has the obvious issue of spreading misinformation (Sonni et al., 2024; Thomson, Thomas and Matich, 2025), however AI writers often miss what is actually newsworthy in a story (Cools and Diakopoulos, 2026) and may perpetuate biases from their training data (Sonni et al., 2024).

AI also makes good journalism harder. There are some AI technologies that may help with identifying false images and text, however in newsrooms today fact checking still uses traditional human processes (Thomson, Thomas and Matich, 2025). There are also less known issues, such as the protection of anonymous sources, a key principle of journalism. Here, the availability of increasingly advanced AI analysis tools threaten this moral principle (Sonni et al., 2024).

From my personal experience, one of the subtle ways generative AI hurts is laziness. The temptation to accept AI output without thorough review is strong. However, this does a great disservice to those who end up consuming the content.

Despite these concerns, I don’t think generative deep learning spells the end of good journalism. Using AI can process complex data into understandable insights and visuals in ways that were previously impossible (Sonni et al., 2024). Combined with the time gained from automating the simple tasks, like weather and sports results (Sonni et al., 2024) we may be entering a new era of sophistication.
